**Prepared by Sahar Pakseresht — application assignment for the Ecosystem & Events Coordinator role**

> iterate, pivot. This proposal is written in that spirit — opinionated,
> concrete, and measurable. It's a first draft of how I'd approach the role, not
> a finished plan. I'd expect the team to tear into it and make it better.

---

## 1. The one thing this strategy optimizes for

**Momentum.** A startup ecosystem is healthy when *more people take more action
more often.* Every event, post, and connection should move someone one step
closer to building something — and bring them back next time.

So the north-star metric I'd propose is not "attendees." It's:

> **Activated participants** = people who show up *and* take a follow-on action
> (apply to Founder Sprint, book a mentor, join the next event, bring a friend,
> start a project).

Attendance fills a room. Activation builds an ecosystem.

---

## 2. Where the leverage is (my read of the situation)

From the role description, Founder School already runs Founder Talks and a range
of events, and wants stronger connections with students, alumni, founders,
mentors, investors, and student organizations. Three patterns tend to hold for
university startup programs:

1. **Events are often one-off, not a funnel.** A great talk ends with applause
   instead of a next step. The value leaks out the door.
2. **The "ecosystem" is a list of contacts, not a living network.** Mentors,
   alumni, and investors are willing to help but aren't given easy, repeated,
   low-effort ways to engage.
3. **Reach is capped by manual, last-minute communications.** Promotion and
   follow-up are the first things to slip when a small team is busy producing.

This strategy attacks all three.

## 3. The model: turn events into a flywheel

```
        ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────┐
        │                                               │
        ▼                                               │
   ATTRACT ──► ACTIVATE ──► CONNECT ──► RETAIN ──► AMPLIFY
   (great        (one clear   (meet the    (come back   (they bring
    events &      next step    right        + go        others &
    promo)        every time)  person)      deeper)     give back)
        ▲                                               │
        └───────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Every event is designed to push people one loop further. The Coordinator's job
is to make the loop spin faster and leak less.

## 4. Three signature initiatives for the first 90 days

### Initiative 1 — "Founder Talks, but the value doesn't leak"

Keep Founder Talks as the flagship, and upgrade the *edges* of the event where
value is usually lost.

- **Before:** a tight promo cadence (T-14 announce, T-7 speaker spotlight, T-2
  reminder, T-0 "doors open"). One owner, one calendar, no last-minute scramble.
- **During:** a student interviewer (great for the interviewer's profile), a
  "who to meet tonight" networking prompt so connections actually happen, and a
  single clear call-to-action on the closing slide.
- **After (the part most people skip):** a recap + photos + speaker's key
  lessons + next-event CTA sent **within 24 hours**, while the energy is hot.

*Why it fits the role:* this is exactly the "planning, producing, coordinating,
communicating, documenting, and measuring" loop the job describes.

### Initiative 2 — "The Ecosystem Engine": make help easy to give

Turn the contact list into a network that engages on autopilot.

- A simple, opt-in **mentor/alumni/investor roster** with what each person is
  happy to help with (e.g. "fundraising," "first hires," "go-to-market").
- **Low-effort, repeatable touchpoints:** a once-a-month "ask me anything"
  slot, a "speaker we need" call-out, a quarterly alumni update they can reply
  to. The bar to contribute should be *one click or one reply.*
- **Reciprocity loop:** every founder helped is later invited to give back. This
  is how a community compounds instead of depleting.

*Why it fits the role:* directly supports "alumni and mentor engagement" and
"collaboration with founders, mentors, investors… around Founder School
activities."

### Initiative 3 — "Founder School is everywhere on campus"

Visibility is a growth channel. Be present where founders already gather.

- **Ambassador pop-ups** at Slush-adjacent events, student org gatherings, and
  Aalto entrepreneurship spaces — a recognizable Founder School presence, not a
  table no one approaches.
- **A repeatable content kit** (poster + Instagram + email templates) so every
  event ships consistent, on-brand promo in minutes. *(The Event Architect tool
  in the attached AI agent is a working prototype of exactly this.)*
- **Cross-promotion deals** with student organizations: we feature their thing,
  they feature ours. Distribution is a trade, not a budget line.

*Why it fits the role:* this is the "be the face of Founder School… ambassador
activities, pop-ups… collaborations with student organizations" mandate.

## 5. How I'd measure it (so we know what's working)

| Layer | Metric | Why it matters | Starting target |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Attract | Registrations & promo reach | Top of funnel | Grow per-event over baseline |
| Activate | **Show-up rate** | Did interest convert? | ≥ 70% of registrations |
| Activate | **Activation rate** (took a next step) | The real signal | ≥ 25% of attendees |
| Connect | New connections / intros made | Network density | Track per event |
| Retain | Repeat-attendance rate | Are we building a community? | Grow month over month |
| Quality | Post-event satisfaction (1–5) | Don't sacrifice quality for scale | ≥ 4.3 |
| Amplify | Referrals + mentor/alumni contributions | Compounding | Trend up |

Each event gets a one-page scorecard. We review monthly, double down on what
works, and kill what doesn't — startup-style.

| Weeks | Focus |

| **1–2** | Learn the team, audit the last few events, talk to 10 students + 5 mentors. Stand up the event scorecard and a shared promo calendar. |

| **7–10** | Stand up the Ecosystem Engine v1 (mentor/alumni roster + first monthly touchpoint). Run the first ambassador pop-up. |
| **11–13** | Review the data, share a transparent "what we learned" recap with the team, and propose the next quarter's bets. |

## 7. Risks & how I'd handle them

- **Over-programming → burnout / thin events.** Fewer, better events beats a
  packed calendar. Protect quality (the 4.3 floor).
- **Activation feels gimmicky.** The "next step" must be genuinely useful, not a
  forced funnel. If it doesn't help the founder, cut it.
- **Small team, big ambition.** Templates, checklists, and the AI agent exist to
  remove repetitive work so human energy goes to relationships — the part that
  can't be automated.
